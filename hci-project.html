Description of the Problem

Laundry is a stressful and often messy affair at Carleton College, where students use shared laundry facilities in their dorm buildings. We are designing an app that will limit the amount of clothes left in the laundry room, prevent lost clothes, make it easier to report issues, and decrease the amount of stress that laundry causes students.


Initial Designs


Addressing User Needs

During the participatory design interview that I conducted to get user input on the design of this app, I learned that the most important features for users are the ability to reserve laundry machines and monitor the situation in the laundry room, so I placed those buttons at the top of the screen and made them prominent. In addition, this led me to include a feature to see, through video cameras, what the status of the laundry room is and a simple graphic to show how many machines are available. My user also described how they would like to report problems in the laundry room, so I made sure to incorporate their requests, including an option to upload pictures and a checkbox if you would like to be contacted further.

UX Reserach Process

We conducted two semi-structured interviews and three participatory design interviews to gather qualitative data to drive our prototyping. The interviewees came from various grade-levels and dorm buildings, providing us with a broad picture of user needs. Add more?

UX Research Analysis

After conducting interviews, we axial coded them, creating an affinity diagram to organize our findings. We sorted the codes into different categories, like Action, Consequence, Condition, and Phenomenon. After doing this preliminary coding, we came up with our final codes and went through all interviews one more time. We generated our final requirements based on the codes the appeared most frequently throughout the interviews.

Final Three Requirements

  1. User should be able to monitor what machines are available in the laundry room at any given time.
  2. User should be able to set a timer to allow them to keep track of when their laundry is done.
  3. User should be able to communicate with other users of the laundry room.

Prototype with Interaction Flows

How Prototype Meets Requirements

My Prototype meets all three requirements that we generated in our research process. The prototype makes it easy to complete the most important tasks: monitoring the laundry room availability, setting timers, and communicating with other users.

The first requirement is met by my first interaction flow. After choosing a dorm building, the user is taken to a page where they can check the availability of machines at the moment. Each machine has its own square, and the squares are red when in use and green when open. There's also a timer that shows the amount of time left on each machine. This allows users to quickly see how many are available and plan out when they can start their laundry.

The second requirement is met by the second interaction flow, where the user sets a timer. The participants we interviewed really wanted an in-app feature that would allow them to set a timer and remind them of their laundry's completion. When on the monitoring page, the user can simply click a button that lets them set a timer.

The final requirement is met by the third interaction flow. Here, the user is able to communicate with other people on issues regarding the laundry room. There is an open chat feature where users can type and send messages. This is dorm-specific to create fewer notifications and clogging in the forum.
